Where to eat

Edinburgh’s culinary scene is rich and diverse — whether you crave a cozy café or elegant fine dining, there's something for every palate and pocket.

Affordable Eats

Oink

Legendary hog roast sandwiches, quick and affordable.

Baba Rista

Cozy café with budget-friendly paninis and great coffee.

Mosque Kitchen

Simple, hearty curry dishes served canteen-style.

Union of Genius

Scotland’s first soup café, warm and wholesome.

Ting Thai Caravan

Legendary Scottish hog roast sandwiches served fast and fresh.

Scenic Cafés

Clarinda’s Tearoom

Quaint tearoom with views of Holyrood Palace.

The Milkman

Rustic coffee shop built into a historic stone stair.

Café at the Palace

Elegant café with palace garden views.

Red Kite Café & Grill

A peaceful café near Arthur’s Seat with outdoor seating.

Black Medicine Coffee Co.

Artistic and atmospheric hideaway café.

Fine Dining

Number One at The Balmoral

Michelin-starred dining with classic decor.

The Witchery by the Castle

Gothic glamour and rich historic charm.

The Dome

Stunning neoclassical building with opulent surroundings.

Ondine

Award-winning seafood restaurant near the Royal Mile.

Restaurant Martin Wishart

French fine dining in Leith with a Michelin star.

Sweet Treats

Mimi's Bakehouse

An award-winning bakery offering indulgent cupcakes, traybakes, and afternoon tea with a cozy, vintage vibe.

Mary’s Milk Bar

Charming retro-style gelato shop near Grassmarket, known for inventive flavors and creamy hot chocolate.

Loudons

Trendy café and bakery offering beautifully plated cakes, scones, and seasonal sweet bites.

The Chocolatarium

An interactive chocolate experience near the Royal Mile where you can learn, taste, and even make your own chocolates.

Sicilian Pastry Shop

A local gem in Leith serving authentic cannoli, cassata, and sweet pastries straight from Sicily.
